Leef Browser — CC0 1.0 Universal

The Leef Browser application and all of its source code are dedicated to the public domain under the Creative Commons CC0 1.0 Universal licence.

What CC0 means in practice

  • You can copy, fork, and modify the code without asking us.
  • You can use it in commercial or proprietary products.
  • You are not required to credit us or include any copyright notices.
  • There are no "viral" clauses — your fork does not have to be open source.

The only thing CC0 doesn't give you is the right to use the Leef name and logo as your own brand. See our Codebase & Fork Policy for the full branding guidelines.

This Website — © 2026 QTech, All Rights Reserved

The source code, design, and content of this website (leefbrowser.site) are copyright © 2026 QTech. This is separate from the browser and is not CC0.

Why the website isn't CC0

This site exists to represent the official Leef project. If its design were freely copyable, it would be trivial for bad actors to clone the site and use it to distribute malware disguised as Leef Browser, or to impersonate QTech to mislead users.

Keeping the website proprietary means we have clear legal standing to request takedowns of impersonation sites.

What you may not do

  • Copy or redistribute this site's code or design.
  • Clone this site to impersonate QTech or Leef Browser.
  • Use modified versions of this site to distribute unofficial or malicious builds.

What you may do

  • Link to this website freely.
  • Quote brief excerpts for journalistic, review, or educational purposes, with attribution.
  • View the source for personal study.

A Note on Copyright

You don't need to register or "apply for" copyright — in Canada and most countries, copyright is automatic the moment something is created. It exists by default.

CC0 is a choice, not the default

When QTech released Leef Browser under CC0, we were voluntarily waiving our automatic copyright on the browser code and placing it into the public domain. That's an active decision, not the default state.

The website, by contrast, retains its automatic copyright because we never chose to waive it. No registration, filing, or special process required — it's just protected.

Reporting Misuse or Impersonation

If you find a website, app, or account impersonating Leef or QTech, please report it to us directly. We take this seriously and will act on it.
